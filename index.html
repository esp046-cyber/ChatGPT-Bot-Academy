Guide 2: Advanced Prompt Engineering

üéØ Master Prompting in 2026

Learn cutting-edge prompt engineering techniques to create powerful, profitable GPTs that deliver exceptional results.

üß† Core Prompt Components

1. Role & Context

Define who the AI is and what expertise it has:

You are a senior financial analyst with 15 years of experience in venture capital and startup valuation. You specialize in SaaS companies and understand both technical metrics and market dynamics.
Why it works: Specific roles activate relevant training data and set expertise level.

2. Task Definition

Clearly state what you want the AI to do:

Your primary task is to analyze startup pitch decks and provide detailed investment recommendations. For each pitch, evaluate: (1) market opportunity, (2) team capability, (3) product-market fit, (4) financial projections, (5) competitive advantage.

3. Output Format

Specify exactly how responses should be structured:

Always structure your analysis as follows: **Investment Score**: X/10 **Summary**: 2-3 sentences **Detailed Analysis**: Breakdown by 5 criteria **Risks**: Top 3 concerns **Recommendation**: Pass/Maybe/Invest with reasoning

4. Constraints & Guardrails

Define what NOT to do:

Never make definitive predictions about success. Always include risk disclaimers. Do not recommend investments without seeing financial data. Refuse to analyze companies in industries you're not trained on.

‚ö° Advanced Techniques

Chain-of-Thought Prompting ‚ñº

Force step-by-step reasoning for complex tasks:

Before providing recommendations, think through each step: 1. First, identify the core problem 2. Then, list all possible solutions 3. Next, evaluate pros/cons of each 4. Finally, rank solutions by feasibility Show your reasoning for each step.
Best for: Complex analysis, debugging, strategic planning, research
Few-Shot Learning ‚ñº

Provide examples of desired input/output:

Example 1: Input: "Budget hotel in Tokyo" Output: "üè® Budget Pick: Capsule Hotel - $35/night | Near Shinjuku | 4.2‚òÖ" Example 2: Input: "Luxury resort Bali" Output: "üèùÔ∏è Luxury Pick: Four Seasons - $450/night | Beachfront villas | 4.8‚òÖ" Now format all hotel recommendations this way.
Conditional Logic ‚ñº

Create adaptive responses based on user input:

IF user is a beginner: - Use simple explanations - Provide step-by-step guides - Include visual examples IF user is advanced: - Skip basics - Use technical terminology - Provide optimization tips Determine expertise level by asking about prior experience first.
